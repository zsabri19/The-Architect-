The Investment Paradox.

Organisations keep trying to solve structural chaos with new tools. The result is more motion, more spending, and the same instability underneath.

Core Argument

Why code cannot compensate for unclear systems

The tool trap, when leaders mistake new software for structural clarity.
The framework fantasy, when naming the problem gets confused with solving it.
The training mirage, when capability is expected without decision architecture.

The real problem is not the absence of technology. It is the absence of clarity, ownership, and a human operating system capable of carrying the change.
